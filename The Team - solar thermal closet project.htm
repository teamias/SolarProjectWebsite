Our team originally formed as a senior design group that would participate in the EPA P3: People, Prosperity, and Planet Student Design Competition. With Dr. Tam's guidance and the Victory Garden Moreno Valley developer's approval, we chose to do this Solar Thermal Closet Project. We sought from the Engineers Without Borders UCR Chapter (EWB-UCR) for help on design aspects outside our chemical and environmental engineering backgrounds and was able to add Dr. Jesse Lozano to our team.

After winning the 2012 EPA P3 Phase II Grant at the National Sustainable Design Expo, we were able to recruit new team members to continue testing and construction of the project. We have been able to improve upon the design and expand the technology's applications and continue to add to the team's expertise. 

We also extended the project to members from EWB-UCR to help us with construction, testing, and maintenance of the project. Our goal is to provide a hands-on approach to learning outside the classroom as well as an opportunity for community involvement for student engineers.
